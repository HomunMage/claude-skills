# Hiring — Interviewer & Hiring Manager

> **Cross-reference**: Also read [interview.md](interview.md) — understanding the candidate's perspective makes you a better interviewer.

## Hiring Philosophy

- Hiring is an **investment decision** — evaluate ROI, not just skill fit
- A bad hire costs far more than a slow hire
- Culture fit ≠ "like me" — it means aligned on values and working style
- The interview is bidirectional: you're also being evaluated

## Pre-Hiring

### Define the Role (before posting)
- What problem does this hire solve? (tie to project pyramid: 目的→願景→設計支柱)
- Must-have vs nice-to-have skills — separate clearly
- Team composition: what's missing? What would complement existing members?
- Budget reality: salary range, equity, benefits

### Job Description
- Lead with what the candidate will **achieve**, not just responsibilities
- Be honest about challenges (filters out poor fits early)
- Avoid wish-list syndrome — 5 requirements max for must-haves

## Interview Design

### Structured Interview
- Same questions for all candidates → fair comparison
- Score rubric defined BEFORE interviews begin
- Mix question types:
  - **Behavioral** (STAR: Situation, Task, Action, Result) — past behavior predicts future
  - **Situational** — hypothetical scenarios relevant to the role
  - **Technical** — verify claimed skills with practical exercises
  - **Values alignment** — how they handle conflict, failure, ownership

### What to Evaluate
- **Problem-solving process** > correct answer
- **Communication clarity** — can they explain their thinking?
- **Self-awareness** — do they know their weaknesses? Do they learn from failure?
- **Ownership mindset** — do they take initiative or wait for instructions?
- **Collaboration** — how do they handle disagreement?

### Red Flags
- Blames others consistently
- Cannot name a specific failure and what they learned
- Answers are vague / no concrete examples
- Overqualified but can't explain why they want this role
- Only talks about individual achievements in team contexts

### Green Flags
- Asks thoughtful questions about the team/product/vision
- Gives credit to teammates
- Shows curiosity and learning trajectory
- Honest about what they don't know
- Demonstrates resilience and iteration mindset

## Post-Interview

### Evaluation
- Score independently before group discussion (avoid anchoring bias)
- Discuss discrepancies — different interviewers notice different things
- Check references: ask about working style, not just skill verification

### Decision Framework
- Would you want this person on your team during a crisis?
- Can they grow into the next level in 1-2 years?
- Does the team get stronger or just bigger?

### Candidate Experience
- Respond to ALL candidates (yes and no)
- Give constructive feedback when possible — builds reputation
- Fast process: every week of delay loses good candidates
