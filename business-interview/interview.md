# Interview — Candidate Preparation

## Interview Philosophy

- An interview is a **mutual evaluation** — you're also deciding if this is the right place for you
- Preparation = confidence, not memorization
- Show your **thinking process**, not just conclusions
- Be honest — pretending to be someone you're not leads to bad matches for both sides

## Preparation Framework

### Research (before interview)
- Company: product, business model, recent news, competitors
- Role: re-read JD, map your experience to each requirement
- Interviewer: LinkedIn background, their work/projects if public
- Prepare 3-5 questions that show you've done research

### Know Your Story
- **Elevator pitch**: who you are + what you do + what you're looking for (30 seconds)
- **Career narrative**: connect the dots — why each transition makes sense
- Build a **story bank** of 8-10 situations covering:
  - A challenging technical problem you solved
  - A conflict with a teammate and how you resolved it
  - A failure and what you learned
  - A time you took initiative / showed ownership
  - A time you had to learn something new quickly
  - A time you disagreed with leadership
  - A project you're most proud of and why
  - A time you had to make a tradeoff under pressure

### STAR Method (for behavioral questions)
- **Situation**: brief context (1-2 sentences)
- **Task**: what was your specific responsibility
- **Action**: what YOU did (not the team — use "I", be specific)
- **Result**: quantifiable outcome + what you learned

## During the Interview

### Communication
- Listen fully before answering — it's OK to pause and think
- Ask clarifying questions if the question is ambiguous
- Structure your answers: "There are three aspects..." → address each
- Be concise — 1-2 minutes per answer, not 5

### Technical Interviews
- Think out loud — the process matters more than the answer
- Start with brute force, then optimize (show iteration mindset)
- Ask about constraints and edge cases before coding
- Test your solution with examples

### Behavioral Interviews
- Use STAR, lead with the punchline when possible
- Be specific: names, numbers, timelines (anonymize if needed)
- Show growth: what would you do differently now?
- Admit what you don't know — then explain how you'd figure it out

### Questions to Ask Them

**Role & Content**
- What does success look like in this role at 3/6/12 months?
- 希望找什麼樣特質的人？
- Why is this role open? What happened to the last person?

**Team**
- 目前團隊規模？打算擴編到多少？
- 使用什麼技術棧/工具？
- How does the team make decisions?
- What's the team's development process? (Agile? Sprints? Vertical slices?)

**Company & Direction**
- 此團隊在公司未來的方向？
- 公司目前最大的困難是什麼？
- 公司對於研發的想法？
- What's the biggest challenge the team is facing right now?

## Post-Interview

- Send a brief thank-you note within 24 hours
- Reflect: what went well, what to improve for next time (AAR mindset)
- If rejected, ask for feedback politely — useful for iteration
- Negotiate offer: research market rate, know your minimum, ask for time to decide

## Common Question Types & Strategy

| Type | Strategy |
|------|----------|
| "Tell me about yourself" | Elevator pitch → career narrative → why here |
| "Why this company?" | Specific reasons tied to your goals + their product |
| "Greatest weakness" | Real weakness + specific steps you're taking to improve |
| "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?" | Growth direction aligned with role, not a rigid plan |
| "Why are you leaving?" | Forward-looking (toward), not backward-looking (away from) |
| Salary expectation | Research range first, give range not single number, defer if early stage |
